neptune ♆

the windiest world

Ice giant

Most of its mass is a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials – water, methane and ammonia.

Short day, long year

Neptune takes about 16 hr to rotate once, and about 165 Earth years to orbit the sun.

Eighth wanderer

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun at a distance of about 2.8B mi.

Moons

Neptune has 14 known moons which are named after sea gods and nymphs in Greek mythology.

Faint rings

Neptune has at least five main rings and four more ring arcs.

Lifeless

Neptune cannot support life as we know it.

uranus ⛢

the sideways planet

Ice giant

Most of its mass is a hot, dense fluid of "icy" materials – water, methane and ammonia.

Short-ish day, longish year

Uranus takes about 17 hr to rotate once, and about 84 Earth years to complete an orbit of the Sun.

Seventh wanderer

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun at a distance of about 1.8B mi.

Moons

Uranus has 27 known moons, they are named after characters from the works of W.Shakespeare and A.Pope.

The other ringed world

Uranus has 13 known rings. The inner rings are narrow and dark and the outer rings are brightly colored.

Lifeless

Uranus cannot support life.

saturn ♄

jewel of our solar system

Gas giant

Saturn is a gas-giant planet and therefore doesn't have a solid surface like Earth’s.

Short day, long year

Saturn takes about 10.7 hr to rotate on its axis once, and 29 Earth years to orbit the sun.

In dim light

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun at a distance of about 886M mi.

Moons

Saturn has 82 moons.

Glorious rings

Saturn has the most spectacular ring system, with seven rings and several gaps and divisions between them.

Lifeless

Saturn can't support life, but some of Saturn's moons have conditions that might support life.

jupiter ♃

twice as massive as all the other planets combined

What's inside

Jupiter is a gas giant and so lacks an Earth-like surface.

Short day/long year

Jupiter rotates once about every 10 hr, but takes about 12 Earth years to complete one orbit of the Sun.

Fifth planet from our star

Jupiter orbits about 484M mi from our Sun.

Moons

Jupiter has more than 75 moons.

Ringed world

In 1979 the Voyager mission discovered Jupiter’s faint ring system.

Lifeless

Jupiter cannot support life. But some of Jupiter's moons have oceans beneath their crusts that might support life.

mars ♂

the red planet

Rugged terrain

Mars is a rocky planet. Its solid surface has been altered by volcanoes, impacts, winds, crustal movement and chemical reactions.

Longer days

One day on Mars takes a little over 24 hr. Mars makes a complete orbit around the Sun in 687 Earth days.

Fourth rock

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun at a distance of about 142M mi.

Moons

Mars has two moons named Phobos and Deimos.

Ringless

There are no rings around Mars.

Lifeless

At this time, Mars' surface cannot support life. Current missions are determining Mars' past and future potential for life.

earth ⊕

our home planet

We're on it

Earth is a rocky planet with a solid and dynamic surface of mountains, canyons, plains and more.

As the world turns

A day on Earth is 24 hr. Earth makes a complete orbit around the sun in about 365 days.

Third rock

Earth is the third planet from the Sun at a distance of about 93M mi.

Moons

Earth has one moon.

Ringless

Earth has no rings.

Home, sweet home

Earth is the perfect place for life.

venus ♀

planetary hot spot

Diverse terrain

Venus' solid surface is a volcanic landscape covered with extensive plains featuring high volcanic mountains and vast ridged plateaus.

A day longer than a year

One day on Venus lasts 243 Earth days.

Second rock

Venus is the second closest planet to the sun at a distance of about 67M mi.

Moonless and ringless

Venus has no moons and no rings.

Greenhouse effect

The planet’s surface temperature is about 465°C.

Lifeless

Venus’ extreme temperatures and acidic clouds make it an unlikely place for life.

mercury ☿

the swiftest planet

Rough surface

Mercury is a rocky planet. It has a solid, cratered surface, much like the Earth's moon.

Long days, short years

One day on Mercury takes 59 Earth days. Mercury makes a complete orbit around the Sun in 88 Earth days.

Insider

It is the closest planet to the Sun at a distance of about 36M mi.

Moonless and ringless

Mercury has no moons and no rings.

Smallest

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system.

Lifeless

It is unlikely life could survive on this planet.
